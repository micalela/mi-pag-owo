Possible Reasons for Tiredness:

  • Rigid Structure:
    Wizard academies often have strict rules and schedules, which can feel stifling to a character who prefers a more spontaneous and free-wheeling approach to magic.

  • Theoretical Focus:
    The emphasis on theoretical knowledge and arcane research might not appeal to a wizard who is more interested in practical applications and hands-on experience.

  • Limited Spell Selection:
    Academies may restrict access to certain types of magic, particularly those considered dangerous or unorthodox. This could frustrate a character who is drawn to forbidden or experimental magic.

  • Monotony of Studies:
    The daily grind of classes, lectures, and spell research can become tedious, even for the most dedicated student.

  • Lack of Adventure:
    The academic life can be isolated from the exciting adventures and challenges that a wizard might seek. This can lead to a desire for something more than just studying.

  • Social Disconnect:
    A wizard might feel out of place or isolated from their peers, particularly if they are more interested in solitary research or have a different personality type than their classmates.

  • Forbidden Magic:
    A wizard might have a deep-seated interest in "forbidden" or "dark" magic, leading them to clash with the academy's rules and potentially face expulsion or exile.
